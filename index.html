FAQs

What is an Accredited Investor?
Generally, to be an Accredited Investor one has to make over $200,000/year for over 2 or more years OR have a net worth over $1,000,000. For more information check out what the SEC says about it.
Who are these investors?
The investors range from established Angel and Venture Capitalists who want invest in their community to high net worth professionals who want to diversify their investment portfolios.
Is Investing risky?
Successfully funding a venture will help it reach its full potential in more ways than the capital it raises. With professionals invested in a local venture, you are untapping a source of evangelists, marketers, promoters, recruiters, and networks that go beyond your reach and increase your chances of success. Keep in mind that businesses are a risky asset and make sure you know the odds of success have been historically low relative to safer altenatives, such as the T-Bill.
How do Business Owners apply?
Contact Us. The only restriction is that you are located in the Massachusetts area. To be successful, show investors why you're business model will be successful and why you're the right person to lead the model to success.
How much equity does a business sell?
That's up to the entrepreneur. VenturePIC ensures that there are Win-Wins for both the owner and the entrepreneur. Both parties will be happy.
I'm great at what I do, but what if I haven't started a business before?
That's OK! VenturePIC goes beyond capital to support business owners. We have a number of services that will help you with legal, accounting, supply chain, and hiring guidance.
